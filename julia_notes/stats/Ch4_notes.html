5.1 Random Sample & Distributions of Statistics

Code 
using Distributions, Random, Plots;
pyplot();

Random.seed!(42)

λ = 1 / 4.5
expDist = Exponential(1 / λ)
n, N = 10, 10^6

means = Array{Float64}(undef, N)
vars = Array{Float64}(undef, N)

for i in 1:N
    data = rand(expDist, n)
    means[i] = mean(data)
    vars[i] = var(data)
end

println("Actual mean: ", mean(expDist), "\nMean of sample means: ", mean(means))

println("Actual variance: ", var(expDist), "\nMean of sample variances: ", mean(vars))

stephist(means, bins=200, c=:blue, normed=true, label="Histogram of Sample Means")
stephist!(vars, bins=600, c=:red, normed=true, label="Histogram of Sample Variances", xlims=(0, 40), ylims=(0, 0.4), xlabel="Statistic value", ylabel="Density")
Actual mean: 4.5
Mean of sample means: 4.4997684419391275
Actual variance: 20.25
Mean of sample variances: 20.24509570642829
Note

After calculating the sample mean and variance, it can be seen that the estimated expected value of the simulated data is a good approximation to the mean and variance parameters of the underlying exponential distribution. That is, for an exponential distribution with rate \(\lambda\), the mean is \(\lambda^{-1}\) and the variance is \(\lambda^{-2}\). The histograms of the sample means and sample variances are using 200 & 600 bins, respectively.

5.2 Sampling from Normla Populations

Its assumed (normality assumption) that the distribution \(F(x; \theta)\) is a normal distribution and hence \(\theta = (\mu, \sigma^{2})\). The distribution of the random variables \(\bar{X}\) and \(\mathit{S}^{2}\) as well as transformations of them are well known. The three relationships play a key role:

\[ \begin{aligned} \bar{X} ~ \text{Normal}(\mu, \sigma^{2}) \\ =& (n - 1)\mathit{S}^{2} \div \sigma^{2} ~ \chi^{2}_{n - 1} \\ =& \text{T} \def \frac{\bar{X}-\mu}{\mathit{S} \div \sqrt{n}} ~ \tau_{n - 1} \\ \end{aligned} \]
